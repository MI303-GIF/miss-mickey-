WHAT IS ERYTHROPOIESIS

The nuclei lose their nucleoli and the structure becomes coarser. The cytoplasm loses its blue colour as the cell mature and granules appear.

Earlier- non-specific azurophilic granules Followed by the specific granules which differentiate the neutrophilic, basophilic and eosinophilic granules.

ERYTHROPOIESIS

1.As cell matures

2.Nuclei become smaller and loses nucleoli

3.Nuclei becomes pyknotic and structureless

4.Then extruded from the cell

5.Cytoplasm matures

6.Dense blue colour replaced by pink staining haemoglobin
